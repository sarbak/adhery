# Scenario 3 (Short): Drop-off Recovery -- Recovering a Churned Patient

**Duration:** ~45 sec | **Turns:** ~14 | **Type:** Outbound recovery call (attempt #5)

---

## Characters

**MIA** (Navigator): Warm, patient, never pushes. Asks permission before probing. Earns the truth through non-pressure.

**MARCUS**: 38, software engineer, stopped semaglutide 6 weeks ago after severe nausea post-dose increase. Guarded at first. Didn't know dose adjustment was an option.

---

## Pre-Call Context

> [SYSTEM] Patient: Marcus Williams, 38. Semaglutide, titrated from 0.25mg to 1.0mg at week 8. Self-discontinued at week 12. Refill not picked up. 4 prior outreach attempts (2 SMS, 2 calls) -- no response. Attempt #5. Adherence status: LAPSED.

---

## The Conversation

**[0:00]**

**MIA:** Hi, this is Mia, an automated care assistant calling from Hims & Hers. Am I speaking with Marcus?

> [SYSTEM] AI disclosure delivered. Recovery outreach attempt #5, day 42.

**MARCUS:** ...Yeah. That's me.

**MIA:** Hey Marcus. I've been trying to reach you. Can I just verify your date of birth?

**MARCUS:** September 12th, 1987.

> [SYSTEM] Identity verified. Proceeding with care discussion.

**MIA:** Thanks. So -- are you still taking the medication?

**MARCUS:** No. I stopped a while ago.

> [SYSTEM] Self-discontinuation confirmed. Permission-based probing initiated.

**[0:15]**

**MIA:** Got it. Totally your call. Was it more about the results, or how you were feeling on it?

**MARCUS:** *(sighs)* How I was feeling. The nausea was... bad. Like, I couldn't function at work. It got worse when they upped the dose. I tried calling my doctor but they were booked out three weeks, so I just stopped.

> [SYSTEM] Root cause surfaced: severe nausea post-dose increase (0.25 -> 1.0mg). Care access barrier: 3-week provider wait. Patient self-discontinued without guidance.

**MIA:** That's -- yeah, that's a tough spot. Can I share one thing? Generally speaking, when side effects get worse after a dose increase, going back to the previous dose is a pretty common adjustment. It's not always up-only.

> [COMPLIANCE] Dose adjustment discussed as general medical fact, not personal recommendation.

**MARCUS:** Wait -- you can do that? Nobody told me that.

> [SYSTEM] Information gap confirmed. Patient unaware of dose reduction option.

**[0:30]**

**MIA:** Yeah. But your provider would be the one to figure out what makes sense for you. Want me to set up a telehealth visit? Usually faster than the office.

> [COMPLIANCE] Clinical question redirected to provider. Telehealth offered as patient's choice.

**MARCUS:** Yeah. Let's do it.

**MIA:** I have Wednesday at 3:15. They'll have the full picture -- what happened, why you stopped, all of it. You won't have to explain from scratch.

> [SYSTEM] Telehealth booked. Pre-visit summary: side-effect-driven discontinuation, dose increase correlation, care access barrier, information gap. Status updated: lapsed -> re-engaging.

**MARCUS:** Thanks for not giving up. I almost didn't pick up.

**MIA:** I'm glad you did. Talk soon, Marcus.

**[END -- 0:45]**
